**Customer Churn Diagnostic & Behavioral Dashboard**
**An End-to-End Analysis of Subscriber Attrition using Microsoft Excel**

📌**Project Overview**
This project investigates a dataset of 6,687 customers to diagnose the drivers behind a 26.86% churn rate. By synthesizing demographic data, regional trends, and competitor behavior, I developed an interactive dashboard to visualize revenue leakage and provide actionable retention strategies.

📊**Key Insights & Findings**
***Demographic Vulnerability:*** The Senior demographic represents the most at-risk group with a 39.09% churn rate, significantly higher than the 'Under 30' segment (23%).
***Competitor Benchmarking:*** 74.5% of churned customers cited better offers or superior devices from competitors as the primary reason for leaving.
***Usage Correlation:*** Customers on data plans of less than 5 GB exhibit the highest churn (34.69%), suggesting a correlation between low-tier plans and low brand loyalty.
***Geographic Hotspots:*** Isolated critical churn rates in California (75%) and Indiana (67%), identifying these as high-priority zones for localized marketing interventions.

🛠️**Technical Implementation**
***Data Cleaning:*** Handled missing values and standardized 20+ churn reasons into actionable categories.
***Advanced Modeling:*** Leveraged Pivot Models and Calculated Fields to cross-reference customer behavior across multiple variables.
***Visualizations:***
1. Geospatial Heat Map: For regional churn intensity.
2. Donut Charts: For high-level demographic segmentation.
3. 3D Bar Charts: For age-based distribution analysis.
4. Sorted Pareto-style Charts: To rank primary churn drivers.

🚀**Strategic Recommendations**
***Retention:*** Implement a "Senior-First" loyalty program to address the 39% churn rate in that segment.
***Competitive Defense:*** Revise pricing and hardware bundling in CA and IN to counter aggressive competitor "Better Offers."
***Plan Optimization:*** Incentivize the transition of users on <5 GB plans to 10 GB+ bundles to increase product "stickiness" and reduce the 34.69% churn rate.
